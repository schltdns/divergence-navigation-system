The Quest for Epistemic Reliability
I’m preparing the formal arXiv submission of the Divergence Navigation System (DNS) — an epistemic evaluation layer that quantifies model disagreement ($\Delta_{div}$) to make uncertainty visible in multi‑model AI systems.

Note: DNS operates in epistemic space (semantic disagreement), not physical robotics or navigation.

Why DNS matters
DNS is a model‑agnostic, training‑free evaluation layer that:

Measures Semantic Divergence: Quantifies the delta ($\Delta_{div}$) between model outputs to reveal latent blind spots.

Implements Falsification: Provides a structured protocol (P1–P6) for hypothesis testing in AI synthesis.

Open Source: Released under the Apache‑2.0 License to foster maximum integration and collaborative growth.

Testable Claim (Falsifiability)
If $\Delta_{div}$ does not correlate with expert‑identified blind spots across domains, the core hypothesis must be revised. We explicitly welcome falsification attempts and rigorous stress-testing — of both the code and the whitepaper.

How you can contribute
I’m opening DNS for co‑authorship and scholarly endorsement to broaden its methodological foundation.

Mathematical / Technical Work: Extensions of $\Delta_{div}$, new similarity metrics, epistemic dispersion modeling.

Methodological Work: Refinement of P1–P6, applications in safety, governance, and didactics.

Case Studies: Applying DNS to specialized domains (e.g., medicine, law, or risk analysis).
